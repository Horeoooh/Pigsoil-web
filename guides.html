Day 1-21 Guide

Basic Swine Manure Composting

Complete 21-Day Step-by-Step Guide for Beginners

About This Method

Traditional aerobic composting using swine waste and plant matter. Perfect for beginners with minimal equipment needed. Creates low-cost organic fertilizer in 21 days.

Materials Needed

Fresh swine manure
Dry plant matter
Rice hulls/sawdust
Shovel or fork
Water source
Tarpaulin cover
1
Initial Setup
Task: Layer swine manure with carbon materials
Phase: Setup Phase

Create alternating layers of swine manure and dry materials:

1. Start with a 6-inch layer of coarse plant material (corn stalks, rice straw)
2. Add 4 inches of fresh swine manure
3. Sprinkle 2 inches of dry materials (rice hulls, sawdust)
4. Repeat layering 3-4 times until pile reaches 1.2-1.5 meters high
5. Water each layer lightly as you build
Setup Phase:
Create alternating layers of swine manure and dry materials. Maintain a 3:1 ratio of brown materials (carbon-rich) to green materials (nitrogen-rich manure).
7
First Turning
Task: Mix all layers thoroughly for proper aeration
Phase: Active Decomposition

Turn pile for oxygen and check moisture levels:

1. Using a pitchfork or shovel, turn the entire pile
2. Move outside materials to the center and center materials to the outside
3. Break up any large clumps
4. Check moisture levels throughout the pile
5. Add water if needed to maintain proper moisture
6. Rebuild pile in same location
Active Decomposition:
Turn pile for oxygen and check moisture levels. After turning, the pile temperature will drop temporarily but should rise again within 24-48 hours.
14
Mid-Point Check
Task: Turn pile and monitor decomposition progress
Phase: Maturation Phase

Monitor texture changes and earthy smell development:

1. Turn the pile completely as done on day 7
2. Add additional dry carbon materials if needed (rice hulls, dried leaves, sawdust)
3. Mix thoroughly to ensure even distribution
4. Adjust moisture if necessary
5. Check for proper brown-to-green ratio
6. Rebuild pile
Maturation Phase:
Monitor texture changes and earthy smell development. This is a good time to adjust the carbon-nitrogen ratio if the pile has been too wet or smelly.
17
Final Turning
Task: Check texture uniformity and adjust moisture
Phase: Pre-Harvest

Final quality checks and preparation for harvest:

1. Perform thorough turning of the pile
2. Check texture uniformity throughout
3. Adjust moisture levels if needed
4. Ensure materials are well-decomposed
5. Check for earthy smell
6. Rebuild pile for final curing
Pre-Harvest:
Final quality checks and preparation for harvest. Materials should be darker, less fibrous, and have a pleasant earthy smell.
21
Harvest Ready
Task: Compost is ready for use or sale
Phase: ✓ Completion

Dark, crumbly compost ready for application or marketplace:

1. Perform final turning of the pile
2. Screen compost through 1/2 inch mesh to remove large particles
3. Return large particles to a new compost pile
4. Test pH (should be 6.5-7.5 for optimal use)
5. Check for proper moisture and crumbly texture
6. Package or store finished compost
7. Store in dry, ventilated area
✓ Completion:
Dark, crumbly compost ready for application or marketplace. Good compost should be dark brown, crumbly, have an earthy smell, and feel cool to the touch.

Hot Composting Method

18-day pathogen-free composting method

THERMOPHILIC PROCESS (55-70°C)

About This Method

Pathogen-free compost from swine manure through high-heat decomposition. This advanced method uses controlled high temperatures to rapidly decompose materials while eliminating harmful pathogens.

Materials Needed

Fresh swine manure
High-carbon materials
Compost thermometer
Turning tools
Water source
Insulation materials
1
High-Carbon Setup
Task: Build pile with proper C:N ratio
Phase: Initial Phase

Create optimal conditions for thermophilic decomposition:

1. Choose a sheltered location with good drainage
2. Create a base layer of coarse materials (3 inches)
3. Mix fresh swine manure with high-carbon materials (2:1 ratio)
4. Build pile rapidly to minimum 1.5m x 1.5m x 1.5m (critical mass)
5. Ensure moisture content is 55-65% throughout
6. Insert thermometer into center of pile
7. Cover with insulating material
Initial Phase:
Create optimal conditions for thermophilic decomposition. Expect pile to reach 40-50°C within 24-48 hours.
3
Temperature Monitor
Task: Check internal heat reaches 60-70°C
Phase: Heating Phase

Monitor temperature rise and maintain optimal conditions:

1. Record temperature using thermometer
2. Check multiple points in the pile
3. Ensure center reaches 60-70°C
4. Verify insulation is properly covering pile
5. Check moisture levels - should feel warm and moist
6. Document temperature readings
7. Adjust covering if heat is escaping
Heating Phase:
Monitor temperature rise and maintain optimal conditions. Target: 60-70°C for pathogen elimination and rapid decomposition.
8
Heat Maintenance
Task: Turn pile to maintain thermophilic phase
Phase: Thermophilic Phase

Sustain high temperatures for rapid decomposition:

1. Record temperature before turning
2. Turn entire pile, moving outer materials to center
3. Ensure all materials reach thermophilic zone
4. Check moisture throughout and adjust as needed
5. Rebuild pile maintaining same dimensions
6. Re-insert thermometer in new center
7. Cover immediately to retain heat
Thermophilic Phase:
Sustain high temperatures for rapid decomposition. Temperature will drop temporarily but should recover within 24 hours.
12
Pathogen Elimination
Task: Continue high-temperature for safety
Phase: Sanitization Phase

Ensure complete pathogen elimination through heat:

1. Document temperature before turning
2. Perform complete turn of all materials
3. Assess decomposition progress - materials should be darker
4. Check for proper texture (less fibrous, more uniform)
5. Test pH levels (should be approaching 6.5-7.5)
6. Adjust moisture if needed
7. Rebuild pile maintaining heat-retention structure
Sanitization Phase:
Ensure complete pathogen elimination through heat. Maintain temperatures above 55°C for thorough sanitization.
18
Curing Complete
Task: Temperature stabilized, ready for harvest
Phase: ✓ Completion

Pathogen-free compost ready for use or marketplace:

1. Screen compost through 1/2 inch mesh
2. Remove any remaining large particles
3. Test final pH and moisture content
4. Package finished compost for storage or use
5. Return oversized materials to new compost batch
6. Document final yield and quality
7. Store in dry, ventilated area
✓ Completion:
Pathogen-free compost ready for use or marketplace. Perfect hot compost is dark brown, crumbly, smells earthy, feels cool, and has uniform texture.
